# Appendix A: Hardware Requirements

## Overview
This appendix provides detailed hardware specifications and requirements for implementing the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics curriculum. The requirements are organized by component type and include both minimum and recommended specifications for different implementation approaches.

## Development Workstation Requirements

### Minimum Specifications
- **CPU**: Intel i5 or AMD Ryzen 5 (6 cores, 12 threads)
- **RAM**: 16 GB DDR4
- **Storage**: 500 GB SSD
- **GPU**: NVIDIA GTX 1060 6GB or equivalent
- **OS**: Ubuntu 22.04 LTS
- **Network**: Gigabit Ethernet, Wi-Fi 802.11ac

### Recommended Specifications
- **CPU**: Intel i9 or AMD Ryzen 9 (8+ cores, 16+ threads)
- **RAM**: 32 GB DDR4/DDR5
- **Storage**: 1 TB NVMe SSD
- **GPU**: NVIDIA RTX 3080/4080 or higher (10GB+ VRAM)
- **OS**: Ubuntu 22.04 LTS
- **Network**: 2.5 GbE Ethernet, Wi-Fi 6E

### High-Performance Specifications (Research/Advanced)
- **CPU**: Intel i9-13900K or AMD Ryzen 9 7950X (16+ cores)
- **RAM**: 64 GB DDR5
- **Storage**: 2+ TB NVMe SSD (multiple drives for parallel I/O)
- **GPU**: NVIDIA RTX 6000 Ada or RTX 4090 (24GB+ VRAM)
- **Additional**: Multiple high-resolution displays for development

## Robot Hardware Platforms

### Unitree Go2 Quadruped (Educational)
- **DOF**: 12 (3 per leg)
- **Processor**: Intel i5-1155G7
- **Sensors**: 3D LiDAR, RGB-D camera, IMU
- **Connectivity**: Wi-Fi, Ethernet, USB-C
- **Battery**: 2h operation time
- **Price**: ~$2,500 USD
- **Use Case**: Locomotion research, perception, navigation

### Unitree H1 Humanoid (Advanced)
- **DOF**: 23 (legs, arms, torso)
- **Processor**: Intel i7 with RTX 3050
- **Sensors**: Multiple cameras, IMU, force/torque sensors
- **Connectivity**: 5G, Wi-Fi 6, Ethernet
- **Battery**: 2.5h operation time
- **Price**: ~$95,000 USD
- **Use Case**: Advanced humanoid research

### Custom Research Platform (DIY)
- **Frame**: Aluminum or carbon fiber construction
- **Actuators**: High-torque servo motors or series elastic actuators
- **Computing**: NVIDIA Jetson Orin AGX or equivalent
- **Sensors**: IMU, cameras, LiDAR, force sensors
- **Power**: 48V battery system
- **Cost**: $15,000 - $50,000 depending on configuration

- **Intel NUC**: Compact, powerful, ROS 2 compatible

## Sensor Hardware

### Vision Systems
- **RGB Cameras**: Intel RealSense D435i, ZED 2i, stereo cameras
- **Thermal Cameras**: FLIR Lepton for thermal perception
- **Event Cameras**: Prophesee for high-speed motion capture
- **LiDAR**: Velodyne Puck, Ouster OS0, Robosense RS-LiDAR

### Inertial Systems
- **IMU**: Bosch BNO055, VectorNav VN-100, ADIS16470
- **GPS**: u-blox NEO-M8N, Septentrio mosaic-X5
- **Magnetometer**: HMC5883L, QMC5883L

### Force and Tactile Sensors
- **Force/Torque**: ATI Gamma, Schunk FT sensors
- **Tactile**: GelSight, BioTac, optical tactile sensors
- **Pressure**: FSR arrays, capacitive pressure sensors

## Communication Hardware

### Network Infrastructure
- **Router**: Enterprise-grade with QoS, multiple bands
- **Switches**: Managed gigabit switches for robotics lab
- **Access Points**: Multiple APs for roaming robotics applications
- **Real-time Networking**: TSN-compatible switches for deterministic communication

### Wireless Technologies
- **Wi-Fi 6E**: High-bandwidth, low-latency communication
- **5G**: For mobile robotics and remote operation
- **Bluetooth**: Short-range device communication
- **UWB**: Precise indoor positioning

## Safety and Infrastructure

### Safety Equipment
- **Emergency Stops**: Multiple E-Stop buttons throughout workspace
- **Safety Barriers**: Physical barriers around robot workspaces
- **Light Curtains**: Safety sensors for human-robot interaction zones
- **Pressure Mats**: Floor sensors for presence detection

### Power Infrastructure
- **UPS Systems**: Uninterruptible power for critical systems
- **Power Distribution**: High-current distribution for robot charging
- **Safety Disconnects**: Emergency power cutoffs
- **Grounding**: Proper electrical grounding for equipment safety

## Laboratory Equipment

### Workshop Tools
- **3D Printers**: Multiple FDM printers for rapid prototyping
- **CNC Machines**: For precision mechanical components
- **Soldering Stations**: For electronics assembly
- **Oscilloscopes**: For electronics debugging
- **Multimeters**: Basic electrical measurements

### Testing Equipment
- **Motion Capture**: OptiTrack or Vicon for precise movement tracking
- **Force Plates**: For gait analysis and balance measurement
- **High-Speed Cameras**: For detailed motion analysis
- **Spectrum Analyzers**: For wireless communication testing

## Cost Considerations

### Budget Implementation (~$10,000)
- Development workstations: 2 x mid-range PCs
- Single research robot platform
- Basic sensor suite
- Essential safety equipment

### Standard Implementation (~$50,000)
- High-performance development workstations
- Multiple robot platforms (2-3)
- Comprehensive sensor suite
- Basic motion capture system
- Advanced safety infrastructure

### Advanced Implementation (~$200,000+)
- Multiple high-end robot platforms
- Complete laboratory setup
- Advanced perception systems
- Full safety infrastructure
- Research-grade equipment

## Procurement Guidelines

### Timing Considerations
- Allow 3-6 months for custom robot procurement
- Standard components: 2-4 weeks
- Research platforms: 6-12 months lead time
- Budget approval and purchasing cycles

### Vendor Selection
- **Academic discounts**: Leverage educational pricing
- **Support requirements**: Ensure adequate technical support
- **Integration capabilities**: Verify ROS 2 compatibility
- **Long-term availability**: Consider component lifecycle

## Maintenance and Support

### Preventive Maintenance
- **Robot calibration**: Monthly calibration of sensors and actuators
- **Software updates**: Regular updates of firmware and software
- **Mechanical inspection**: Quarterly inspection of joints and linkages
- **Battery replacement**: Annual battery replacement for mobile robots

### Support Infrastructure
- **On-site support**: Trained technicians for basic maintenance
- **Remote support**: Vendor support for complex issues
- **Spare parts**: Maintain critical spare parts inventory
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive maintenance documentation

## Environmental Requirements

### Workspace Requirements
- **Floor space**: Minimum 4m x 4m for humanoid robot operation
- **Ceiling height**: Minimum 3m for full robot operation
- **Ventilation**: Adequate cooling for computing equipment
- **Lighting**: Adjustable lighting for vision systems

## Summary
The hardware requirements for Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics span multiple domains from development workstations to robot platforms to laboratory infrastructure. The specific requirements will vary based on the implementation approach, budget, and research objectives. Careful planning of hardware procurement is essential for successful implementation of the curriculum.

The investment in appropriate hardware enables students to gain hands-on experience with real robotic systems, which is crucial for understanding the practical challenges of physical AI. Consider starting with a minimum viable setup and expanding based on experience and requirements.
